If you own a modern iPhone and have ever tried to transfer a picture to your Windows computer, you’ve probably run into a wall: the dreaded **.HEIC** file extension. 

By default, iOS devices capture photos in **High Efficiency Image Format (HEIF/HEIC)**. While this format is incredible for saving storage space on your phone while maintaining high image fidelity, it is notoriously difficult to work with outside of the Apple ecosystem.

Many websites, social media platforms, older software, and Windows default photo viewers simply refuse to open HEIC files. You need them in a universally supported format like **JPG (JPEG)** or **PNG**.

Here is exactly how to convert your HEIC photos securely, locally, and for free.

## Why are HEIC Files so Hard to Open?

Apple adopted HEIC to solve a major problem: our smartphones were taking increasingly higher resolution photos, which rapidly filled up 64GB and 128GB local storage capacities. Advanced compression algorithms in HEIC allow photos to take up about **half the space** of a standard JPG while looking just as good.

However, HEIC is heavily patented, which restricts its free native adoption across platforms like Windows or older Android software.
